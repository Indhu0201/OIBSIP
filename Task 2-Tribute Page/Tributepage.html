Tribute to Kalpana Chawla

"An astronaut who touched the stars and inspired millions."

Biography

Kalpana Chawla was born on March 17, 1962, in Karnal, Haryana, India. From an early age, she was fascinated by aircraft and space exploration, often sketching planes and dreaming of flying beyond the skies.

She graduated in Aeronautical Engineering from Punjab Engineering College and moved to the United States in 1982. She earned her Master’s degree in Aerospace Engineering from the University of Texas at Arlington, followed by a Ph.D. from the University of Colorado Boulder.

Kalpana joined NASA in 1988 and was selected as an astronaut candidate in 1994. She flew on two space missions: STS-87 in 1997, becoming the first Indian-born woman in space, and STS-107 in 2003, a mission tragically cut short during re-entry.

Achievements & Legacy

  • First Indian-born woman to travel to space.
  • Recognized for her contributions to aerospace engineering and space exploration.
  • Honored posthumously with awards including the Congressional Space Medal of Honor.
  • Institutions, scholarships, and satellites named in her honor continue to inspire future generations.

Legacy

Kalpana Chawla is remembered not just for her space missions but for symbolizing courage, dedication, and ambition. Her story motivates young people, especially women, to pursue careers in science and technology.

Her life’s work and tragic passing during the Space Shuttle Columbia disaster on February 1, 2003, have made her an enduring icon of perseverance and pioneering spirit.
